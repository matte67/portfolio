## The concept

RE:WILD turns biodiversity from a topic observed on a screen into a physical, shared system. One to thirty participants enter an ecosystem in crisis and use touch, drag, swipe, and multi-touch interactions across the room’s walls.

The project is currently a pitch and game design document, not an implemented or playable game. Matteo independently developed the concept, mechanics, interaction model, art direction, educational positioning, and business proposal.

The group succeeds by repairing relationships rather than collecting isolated rewards. Habitats, species, resources, and environmental events react to the players’ decisions.

## Gameplay structure

The session moves through briefing, exploration, coordinated action, a critical event, final rebalancing, and a teaching debrief. This gives the installation the structure of a game rather than treating interactivity as a visual spectacle.

## Important decisions

- Make cooperation a rule of the system, not an optional social layer.
- Let every intervention change a connected ecological model.
- Support a full classroom without personal controllers or individual devices.
- End with an explicit debrief linking choices to species, mistakes, and concepts learned.

## Challenges and trade-offs

Supporting up to thirty players increases physical accessibility and coordination challenges. Immediate feedback must remain legible across a room, while the simulation must be understandable without becoming scientifically trivial. The game also needs to separate shared success from individual scoring.

## Outcome and next validation

The current material establishes the concept, mechanics, interaction modes, art direction, educational market, and an initial business roadmap. No implementation or classroom validation is claimed. The next meaningful milestone would be a tested room-scale prototype followed by classroom observation and learning-outcome evaluation.
